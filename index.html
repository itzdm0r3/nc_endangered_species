Save The Endangered Species Of North Carolina


Introduction

The State of North Carolina is home to numerous endangered species. As of July 2016, 35 animals have been included on the list. Animals such as the Carolina Nothern Flying squirrel who make their home in the state have found themselves on the brink of extinction.

According to a report by the U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service, the habitats important to species like the Flying squirrel are under threat from environmental factors such as: pests, pollution, development, and climate change. If you would like to read a copy of the report click here.

Due to the continued raise in population throughout the Southern Appalachians, the displacement of these species is occuring at such an alarming rate that futher studies are taking place to see if recovery is even possible.

The purpose of this webpage is to spread awareness of the growing issue of species becoming endangered in North Carolina (and its surrounding areas) due to the above mentioned environmental factors.


Species Endangered Throughout North Carolina

Animals on the list include the some of the following:

  • Carolina nothern flying squirrel
  • Virgina big-eared Bat
  • Appalachian elktoe
